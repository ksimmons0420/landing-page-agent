# SEO + GEO Copy Formulas

Page title and meta description formulas optimized for both traditional Google Search (SEO) AND generative AI search engines like ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews (GEO).

## Title Tag Formula

`{Primary Keyword} — {Specific Number/Offer} | {Brand}`

**Targets:**
- ≤60 characters for full display in Google SERP (desktop + mobile)
- ≤70 characters absolute hard cap (Google may truncate visible portion but indexes all keywords)

**Structure:**
1. **Primary keyword first** — service + city for local businesses (e.g. "Las Vegas Artificial Turf"). Front-loading boosts SEO + matches scan pattern in SERP.
2. **Specific number/offer** — exact dollar amount, year founded, percentage, rating. Numbers in titles boost CTR ~30% per Google's display tests.
3. **Differentiator or CTA** — "Lifetime Warranty", "Free Estimate", "Same-Day Service".
4. **Brand last** — for branded-search recognition. Shorter brands save more chars.

**Examples (for service-area businesses):**

```
Las Vegas Artificial Turf — $4.99/sq.ft + Lifetime Warranty | US Turf  (69 chars)
Phoenix HVAC Repair — Same-Day Service, 4.8★ | Reliable Air            (64 chars)
Denver Roofing — Free Estimate, Storm Damage Specialist | Mile High    (68 chars)
San Diego Pool Cleaning — $99/Mo, Family-Owned Since 1998 | AquaPros   (68 chars)
```

**What NOT to do:**
- ❌ "{Brand} - Your Local {Service} Provider" — generic, no keyword, no offer
- ❌ "Welcome to {Brand}" — wastes title on unhelpful intro
- ❌ "{Service} Services in {City}, {State}, USA" — over-stuffed, sounds spammy

## Meta Description Formula

`{Authority/Longevity Hook}. {Service+Location} from {Price}, {Differentiator}, {Dollar-Value Benefit}. {Star Rating} · {Review Count} {Source}.`

**Targets:**
- 130-160 characters total
- All key info in first 120 characters (mobile snippet truncates earlier)

**Structure:**
1. **Authority hook (sentence 1)** — "Family-owned [city] [service] since [year]." Establishes credibility immediately + GEO-friendly cite-able statement.
2. **Offer specifics (sentence 2)** — comma-separated list of concrete numbers: starting price, financing terms, rebate amount, warranty length.
3. **Trust + verification (sentence 3)** — star rating · review count, source (Google/Yelp/BBB). Optional CTA at end if char count allows.

**Examples:**

```
160 chars exact:
"Family-owned Las Vegas artificial turf installer since 2003. Starting at $4.99/sq.ft, lifetime warranty, $7/sq.ft water rebates. 4.7★ across 472 Google reviews."

152 chars (CTA-led variant):
"Las Vegas's trusted artificial turf installer since 2003. From $4.99/sq.ft, lifetime warranty, $7/sq.ft water rebate. 4.7★ · 472 reviews. Free estimate."

150 chars (offer-led variant):
"Las Vegas artificial turf from $4.99/sq.ft + 0% financing for 18 months. Lifetime warranty. $7/sq.ft water rebate. Family-owned since 2003. 4.7★ Google."
```

## GEO-Specific Rules

AI search engines (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews, Claude) cite content based on:

### Citation triggers
- **Specific numbers** — rating, review count, price, year, percentage, dollar amount, count of installations
- **Authority markers** — "BBB Accredited", "Licensed [State] Contractor", "[Award] winner", "[Year] founded"
- **Declarative cite-able statements** — "X is a family-owned [service] in [city] since [year]" reads as a fact
- **Freshness signals** — current year mentioned, "as of [date]", recent updates
- **E-E-A-T markers** — Experience (years in business), Expertise (certifications), Authoritativeness (partnerships, awards), Trust (reviews, BBB)

### Pack the meta description with cite-able facts
For "best [service] in [city]" type AI queries, the AI will cite a few sources. Your meta description is one of the first signals it reads. Numbers in the description = quotable facts.

A meta with `4.7★ · 472 Google reviews · since 2003 · BBB Accredited` gives the AI 4 distinct cite-able facts. A meta with `we provide quality service` gives 0.

### Content body should reinforce meta facts
The page's body content should repeat and elaborate on every fact mentioned in the meta. AI engines cross-reference meta claims against page content for trust scoring.

## Worked Example — US Turf

**Inputs gathered:**
- Service: artificial turf installation
- City: Las Vegas
- Specific offer: $4.99/sq.ft starting price
- Differentiator: lifetime warranty
- Founded: 2003 (since 2003 = 23 years experience)
- Rating: 4.7★
- Reviews: 472 Google reviews
- Bonus: $7/sq.ft Vegas water rebate, 0% financing 18 months
- Authority: BBB Accredited, Water Smart Contractor, VGK Official Partner, Best of Las Vegas 2021/2022/2025

**Picked title:**
```
Las Vegas Artificial Turf — $4.99/sq.ft + Lifetime Warranty | US Turf  (69 chars)
```

**Picked meta:**
```
Family-owned Las Vegas artificial turf installer since 2003. Starting at $4.99/sq.ft, lifetime warranty, $7/sq.ft water rebates. 4.7★ across 472 Google reviews.  (160 chars)
```

**Why this combo wins:**
- Both lead with city + service (primary keyword for SEO)
- Both pack concrete numbers (4.99, 7, 4.7, 472, 2003)
- Title focuses on offer hook for click-through
- Meta focuses on authority + numbers for AI citation worthiness
- All key info in first 120 chars of meta (mobile-safe)
- Brand at end of title for branded-search recognition

## A/B Test Variants

If running variants in your CMS or analytics:

**CTA-led title (may convert better in branded search):**
```
Artificial Turf {City} — Free Estimate, ${price}/sq.ft | {Brand}  (≤64 chars)
```

**Authority-led title (may rank better for "best X in Y"):**
```
{City} Artificial Turf Since {Year} — {Star}★ · {Reviews}+ Reviews | {Brand}  (~70 chars)
```

**Offer-led meta (better for purchase-intent queries):**
```
{Service} in {City} from ${price}/sq.ft + 0% financing for {months} months. {Differentiator}. ${rebate}/sq.ft {rebate-name}. Family-owned since {year}. {star}★ Google.
```
