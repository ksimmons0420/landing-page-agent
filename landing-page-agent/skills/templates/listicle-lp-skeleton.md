# Listicle LP — Skeleton + Operator Checklist

Quick-start structure for a listicle landing page. Pull this BEFORE designing any listicle (especially "Best X for Y" affiliate / lead-gen / SEO pages).

## Why this exists

Listicle LPs are one of the most reliable formats across verticals (DTC, lead-gen, affiliate, SaaS). But 5-8 thin items lose to 10-20+ comprehensive lists with methodology + author bio + per-item CTAs. The format that worked in 2022 doesn't work in 2026.

## Item count by intent

| Intent | Item count | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Paid funnel** (Meta/Google ads → LP) | 5–10 curated items | Conversion-focused. Above-fold winner pick + brief alternatives. |
| **SEO + GEO crossover** (organic search) | 10–20+ comprehensive | E-E-A-T + methodology + author bio mandatory for AI search citations. |
| **Affiliate** | 8–15, "Best for X" categorized | Each item has a different "best for" angle (Best for budget · Best for power users · Best for beginners). |
| **AI-search-optimised** (Seer Interactive 2025-2026 data: ~30% MoM listicle citation decline for thin lists Nov-Jan) | 10–20+ comprehensive | Thin 5-8 item lists are getting de-cited. Comprehensive with methodology wins. |

## Hero formula

```
Headline      — "The 12 Best [X] for [Y] in 2026"
Sub           — Methodology one-liner ("We tested [N] [products] over [duration].")
Visual        — Hero image OR top-3 winner cards
Primary CTA   — "Jump to our #1 pick →" (anchor to top item)
Methodology   — Below the hero, ≤3 sentences (what tested, how scored, who's the author)
```

## Item card structure (each one)

```
[Number badge]
Headline       — Brand + product name
Best for       — One-liner ("Best for budget-conscious creators")
Image          — Product hero shot
Pros (3-4)     — bullet
Cons (1-2)     — bullet (yes, even on your own product — credibility)
Per-item CTA   — "Check price →" (NOT "Check on Amazon" — "Check price" beats it 2x in tests)
```

## Non-negotiables for 2026

1. **E-E-A-T markers** — Author bio with credentials, methodology disclosure, real-world testing time.
2. **Mobile vertical stack** — Items render as full-width cards on mobile, NOT as a grid.
3. **Sticky CTA** — Bottom bar with "See top pick" on mobile, persistent regardless of scroll depth.
4. **Schema.org `Product` + `Review`** — Required for rich snippets and AI-search citations.
5. **Methodology section** — Even 100 words is better than nothing. "We spent 80 hours testing X products from Y vendors during Z timeframe."
6. **Updated date** — "Last updated: [date]" near hero. AI search penalises stale content.
7. **Comparison table** — Optional but high-converting. Above the cards or after the methodology.

## Content lengths by vertical

| Vertical | Target word count | Why |
|---|---|---|
| DTC impulse | 1,000–1,800 | Quick decision, low consideration |
| Lead-gen (services) | 2,000–3,500 | Trust-building, more education |
| Affiliate (commerce) | 2,500–4,000 | SEO + conversion both matter |

## CTA strategy

- **Per-item CTA:** "Check price →" (winner) > "Check on Amazon" (2x worse) > "Buy now" (3x worse on cold traffic)
- **Sticky CTA:** "See our top pick" or "Jump to #1" — anchors to top item
- **End-of-page CTA:** Repeat winner + a "still not sure?" secondary CTA to a comparison page

## Reference LPs to dissect

- **Wirecutter** — gold standard for methodology + E-E-A-T
- **NerdWallet** — best-in-class for fintech listicles
- **Forbes Advisor** — strong "Best for X" categorization
- **MoneyUnder30** — comparison table done right
- **ConvertFlow templates** — modern listicle layout components

## Pre-flight checklist (apply BEFORE shipping)

- [ ] Item count matches intent (5-10 paid / 10-20+ SEO)
- [ ] Each item has: number, brand, "best for", image, pros, cons, CTA
- [ ] "Check price" used as per-item CTA (not "buy" or vendor name)
- [ ] Mobile = vertical stack, NOT grid
- [ ] Sticky CTA pinned on mobile
- [ ] Schema.org Product + Review markup
- [ ] Methodology paragraph above the list
- [ ] Author bio with credentials
- [ ] "Last updated: [date]" visible
- [ ] Comparison table present (recommended)
- [ ] No item has only pros (every item gets at least one con — credibility)
- [ ] Top item has clear "winner" treatment (gold border / "best overall" badge)

## Anti-patterns

❌ **Thin 5-item list with no methodology** — losing AI-search citations as of 2025-2026.
❌ **All items have the same pros/cons** — reads as fake, kills credibility.
❌ **Vendor-specific CTAs above per-item** — "Buy on Amazon" lifts trust loss vs "Check price".
❌ **No author bio** — modern AI search downranks anonymous content.
❌ **Mobile grid** — items get squished, scroll depth tanks.
❌ **No comparison table** — leaves easy CR on the table.
